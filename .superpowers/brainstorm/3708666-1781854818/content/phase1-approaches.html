Phase 1 Non-Crossing Construction Options

Goal: preserve current aggregate diversity while making self-crossing structurally impossible, or as close as practical, before Phase 2.

A. Star-Shaped Radial Profile

Sample angle bins around a center, choose one radius per bin, smooth the polar radius signal, then convert to points. Simple by construction if radius stays positive and angles are monotone.

B. Convex Hull + Safe Dents

Build a hull, then insert candidate interior points only when the replacement path stays inside the local triangle/visibility cone. Simple by construction with richer concavity than a pure radial loop.

C. Planar Graph Simple Cycle

Build a planar graph, such as Delaunay or visibility-filtered edges, then choose a simple cycle. Graph-theoretically natural, but cycle selection can be awkward to make diverse and GPU-friendly.

Recommendation

Start with B: Convex Hull + Safe Dents. It gives a structural simplicity invariant like C wants, keeps the candidate-pool spirit, and can preserve concavity better than a purely star-shaped radial profile. Use A as the fallback/control because it is the cleanest proof of simplicity and easiest later Warp port.
