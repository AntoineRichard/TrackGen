\section{Review Protocol}
\label{sec:review-protocol}

\subsection{Review Questions and Scope}

This survey asks: (i) how are robot courses represented and generated; (ii)
which constraints, validity checks, and evaluation measures make generated courses
usable for learning and control; and (iii) how can these choices be reported so
that results can be compared and reproduced? The unit of technical interest is a
course layout that constrains traversal: a track or corridor, road, ordered gate
or waypoint sequence, buoy course, or world layout. We consider such layouts for
ground, aerial, and maritime robots, including simulated systems when vehicle
model and control make course geometry consequential. A transferable adjacent
method is eligible only when its source exposes a representation, constraint,
metric, or interface that can be applied to a robot course without reinterpreting
the source output or objective.

The scope is deliberately broader than the historical state-of-the-art document,
which focused on track- and road-like geometry generation. It also covers
course distributions for robot learning and control, gate and waypoint courses,
simulation and benchmark interfaces, official specifications and competition
materials, and adjacent procedural road, map, network, level, and test-input
generation. Conversely, a racing-line planner, controller, traffic generator,
or appearance/dynamics randomizer is not a course-generation method merely
because it operates on a track.

\subsection{Discovery, Reports, and Works}

Searches were executed on 2026-06-29, with full-text and version verification
continuing through 2026-07-06. A prespecified matrix defined 25 query families,
and the search log records 249 executed searches. The streams were: historical
seed corpus; independent ground search; independent aerial/maritime search;
citation-informed geometry/RL expansion; and simulator, benchmark, and
official-artifact search. We searched the seed corpus and mixed primary-web
surfaces, including publisher, repository, standards, and project pages; we do
not infer coverage of any unnamed bibliographic database. Exact-title and DOI
follow-ups, together with forward and backward citation expansion, resolved
candidate reports and related artifacts. The machine-readable query matrix and
search log are provided as Supplementary Data \texttt{search\_queries.csv} and
\texttt{search\_log.csv}.

Discovery assembles a candidate population rather than evidence for a technical
claim; primary reports and authoritative artifacts are assessed during screening.

\paragraph{Supplementary contextual search.}
After the main corpus freeze, we conducted a supplementary contextual search on
2026-07-06 to position this review against adjacent surveys. Targeted categories
were autonomous vehicle and drone racing, search-based procedural content
generation, safety-critical driving scenario generation, and procedural virtual
world generation. We verified candidate surveys through DOI and publisher landing
pages, arXiv records, author or institutional publication pages, and citation
index records. These surveys are used only for reader-facing field and
literature-gap context: they were not pooled into method-prevalence summaries or
submitted to Pass~2 contribution coding. Claims drawn from this search are
therefore limited to the stated scope, taxonomy, and positioning of the inspected
surveys, not to quantitative characterization of the main evidence corpus.

The screening unit is a \emph{report}: one identifiable article, preprint,
thesis, technical report, standard, dataset, release, repository revision, or
official document. Exact duplicate reports are collapsed with their aliases
retained. Versioned or companion reports remain linked until their unique
evidence has been assessed. The synthesis unit is the underlying \emph{work}:
linked reports are grouped by stable identifiers, authorship, artifact identity,
and contribution overlap; a canonical citable report is selected, and a work is
counted at most once in any later quantitative synthesis.

\subsection{Two-Pass Evidence Procedure}

Pass~1 is a binary, evidence-based retention screen. A report is retained only
when the frozen packet establishes at least one of: a direct course-generation
contribution; a fixed-course property that transfers to a survey or benchmark
design decision; or contextual evidence from an inspected survey or systematic
review. Pass~2 is separate multi-label contribution coding for evidence tier,
method, representation, interface, dataset, benchmark, validity test, and
metric. A retained-report count is therefore neither a method count nor a
prevalence estimate.

Fixed routes, static competition layouts, and contextual works are retained only
when they provide citable transferable representation, interface, benchmark,
evaluation, or terminology context. They are not relabelled as generators.
In particular, running, optimizing, or evaluating control on a fixed route does
not establish a course-generation contribution.

Technical claims require inspected primary reports or authoritative companion
artifacts. Version-identified official documentation may support artifact,
interface, or benchmark claims; directly inspected reviews may support only
field, terminology, or literature-gap context. Each deciding item records a
version, retrieval date, canonical source, and precise page, section, figure,
algorithm, repository path/line, or stable documentation anchor. Titles, search
snippets, and unverified abstract inferences are not material evidence;
abstract-only records cannot be included.

\subsection{Independent Screening and Adjudication}

Each report is rated independently twice against the same versioned,
frozen evidence set. A calibration sample was screened before the main phase,
and paired ratings were concealed until locked. Adjudication reconciles status,
criterion, reason-text, and unresolved-evidence conflicts against the frozen
source record. The detailed adjudication taxonomy, provisional dispositions,
and audit trail are reported in Supplementary Data.
