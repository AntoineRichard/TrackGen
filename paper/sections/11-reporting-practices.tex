\section{Proposed Reporting Standard and Pending Empirical Audit}
\label{sec:reporting-practices}

This section proposes a reporting standard; it does not report a prevalence audit.
Generator, course, simulator, and policy claims remain separate. The course card and
conformance fields are defined normatively in \cref{sec:benchmark-protocol}; the
measures and denominators are defined in \cref{sec:metrics}.

\begin{table}[t]
  \centering
  \caption{Compact proposed reporting standard.}
  \label{tab:reporting-standard}
  \small
  \begin{tabularx}{\linewidth}{@{}>{\raggedright\arraybackslash}p{0.25\linewidth}Y@{}}
    \toprule
    Record & Minimum disclosure \\
    \midrule
    Generator card & Version, configuration, seeds, domain/vehicle envelope, all
    attempts and failures, validity definition, raw/repair status, runtime, compute,
    memory, and artifact hashes \\
    Course and conformance records & Course card required by
    Section~\ref{sec:benchmark-protocol}; export/import outcome, units/frames,
    collision/reset checks, semantic loss, and observation/action contract \\
    Policy evaluation record & Algorithm/configuration, all training and rollout
    seeds, suite and simulator hashes, split/leakage checks, outcome taxonomy,
    denominators, uncertainty, interactions, steps, wall time, and memory \\
    Archival disclosure & Source or executable environment, licenses, provenance,
    access restriction, and released versus proposed versus unavailable status \\
    \bottomrule
  \end{tabularx}
\end{table}

A future empirical audit will use retained, sufficiently accessible sources as its
denominator for each item. It will code reported, not reported, unclear, or not
applicable separately; neither access failure nor a missing locator is evidence of
non-reporting. Required fields and locators are drawn from the evidence, claims, and
metrics ledgers, including source version, page/section or artifact locator, coding
rationale, and applicability.

No counts, citations, or literature-prevalence conclusions are made until that coding is
populated and reviewed.
