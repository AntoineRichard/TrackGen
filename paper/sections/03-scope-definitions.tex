\section{Scope, Definitions, and Boundaries}
\label{sec:scope-definitions}

A course is an ordered spatial specification of the route or traversal constraints for
a robot-racing task. A representation encodes that course, while a generator constructs,
selects, modifies, replays, or repairs represented courses. A training distribution is
the population and sampling rule used during system development, and an evaluation
suite is a fixed or reproducibly sampled set used to assess behavior.
