\section{Current Implementation Coverage and Release Gaps}
\label{sec:reference-implementations}

This author-maintained source-tree audit covers commit
\nolinkurl{398d136b0c6038971a610dfb4455b392f623397f}. In that audit, 124 RL
utility tests, 63 Phase~1 tests, and 51 Phase~2 tests passed. These are focused
passing-test counts for the audited surfaces, not evidence of broader correctness,
a released benchmark, or a complete reference implementation.

The track registry contains the six rows in \cref{tab:current-phase-one-generators}.
The separate gate-generation registry contains five generators---bezier, hull, polar,
voronoi, and checkpoint---and does not contain repulsive. The five fixed-shape Warp
track generators support CUDA graph capture; repulsive follows a distinct Phase~1
Sobolev procedure and runs eagerly with host control on CUDA. Fourier remains an
unregistered experimental Torch-only method.

Construction uses a fixed configured batch, per-environment seeds, and a shared fixed
configuration. \texttt{TrackGenerator} and \texttt{GateGenerator} respectively reuse
persistent mutable \texttt{Track} and \texttt{GateSequence} buffers across calls;
selected-environment regeneration is not supported. The audit makes no cross-device
bit-identity claim.

The production Phase~2 order is generator-local selection or fallback,
constant-spacing resampling, XPBD geometric projection, inflation, and final
geometric validity. Energy and tangent-point methods are comparison or test oracles,
not the production runtime; they should not be conflated with repulsive's separate
Phase~1 Sobolev procedure. Intermediate raw, pre-projection, and post-projection
buffers are private, and no public raw-to-derived lineage is retained. Neither
$N_{\max}$ truncation events nor broadphase cache-overflow counters are exposed.
Geometric validity alone does not establish dynamic or simulator feasibility.

\paragraph{Course.}
The facade provides whole-batch generation, per-environment progress reset, and stable
buffers. \texttt{Course} is not a Gymnasium \texttt{Env}: it defines no actions,
observations, reward, or termination, and does not own external localizers or props.

\paragraph{Checkpoints and progress.}
Utilities expose crossings, laps, wrong-way and wrong-checkpoint flags, and distance to
the next checkpoint. These are signals not rewards.

\paragraph{Collision.}
Oriented boxes query exact boundary segments or an approximate signed-distance field;
discs represent posts. These are geometric queries not physics or contact response.

\paragraph{Localization and speed.}
Utilities expose $(s,n)$ coordinates, nearest segments, curvature, and speed-profile
proxies. Warm starts have limits and require reset after teleport or regeneration;
these proxies do not establish dynamic feasibility.

\paragraph{Props.}
Props provide rendering-only poses and are not colliders unless explicitly connected
to the disc checker.

There is no Gymnasium or mainstream simulator/RL adapter, \texttt{CourseSpec}
serialization, manifest/hash lineage, or immutable corpus. The implementation is a
non-comparative current snapshot, distinct from the future-facing protocol in
\cref{sec:benchmark-protocol}.
