\section{Preregistered Research Hypotheses}
\label{sec:open-problems}

These are preregistered hypotheses, not evidence-ranked gaps. No hypothesis is
evidence-ranked until Pass 2 is complete. Each comparison uses the measures in
\cref{sec:metrics} and immutable identifiers, lineage, and adapters from
\cref{sec:benchmark-protocol}.

\paragraph{H1: Cross-domain representation.}
Compare source and imported predicates for ground, aerial, and maritime
\texttt{CourseSpec} instances. Reject common-core adequacy when a required
domain constraint is lost. Required artifacts are source/derived IRs, extension blocks,
export traces, and predicate-level conformance records.

\paragraph{H2: Geometry versus control feasibility.}
Estimate the matched-stratum difference between geometry/topology yield and
dynamics/control-aware yield for a declared envelope. Reject geometry-only adequacy
when the preregistered gap bound is exceeded. Required artifacts are vehicle/controller
versions, budgets, initial states, solver outcomes, and raw attempts.

\paragraph{H3: Difficulty calibration.}
Compare descriptor-stratum ordering across reference agents, seeds, and simulators.
Reject policy-independent calibration when rank stability falls below its preregistered
bound. Required artifacts are frozen reference distributions, descriptor code, policies,
and paired outcomes.

\paragraph{H4: Representative versus adversarial generation.}
Compare held-out distribution distance, coverage, duplicates, and policy failures under
matched budgets. Reject representative-generation claims when adversarial improvement
violates fixed distributional bounds. Required artifacts are target/reference corpora,
candidate pools, selection logs, and policy evaluations.

\paragraph{H5: UED, curriculum, and repair bias.}
Compare co-adapted policies and frozen generator snapshots on common held-out suites;
separately estimate paired raw-to-repaired descriptor and outcome shifts. Reject
transfer or repair-neutrality when the respective preregistered bounds fail. Required
artifacts are snapshot lineage, raw/derived pairs, repair traces, and common IDs.

\paragraph{H6: Cross-generator, cross-simulator, and sim-to-real transfer.}
Estimate a crossed generator-by-simulator transfer matrix and compare generated
descriptor distributions with a fixed, conditioned real reference corpus. Reject a
transfer claim on material off-diagonal loss or real-reference mismatch beyond the
preregistered interval. Required artifacts are corpus extraction/conditioning, adapters,
conformance records, and paired policies.

\paragraph{H7: Recoverability.}
Compare recovery probability across perturbation distributions, controllers, and
budgets. Reject a recovery claim when safe-set or recovered-set occupancy misses its
bound, or collision/irreversible failure exceeds its ceiling. Required artifacts are
perturbation draws, horizons, safe/recovered definitions, trajectories, and failure
taxonomy.

\paragraph{H8: Multi-agent and overtaking behavior.}
Compare collision, deadlock, legal-pass, illegal-pass, and completion rates across
roles and interaction rules. Reject a passing or interaction claim when separation or
legal-pass bounds fail. Required artifacts are role assignments, initial states, pass
rules, interaction traces, and outcome labels.

\paragraph{H9: Card, reconstruction, and audit agreement.}
Compare course-card declarations with reconstruction and conformance results, then
measure agreement between preregistered audit codes and their cited locators. Reject
completeness when a required card field, reconstruction, conformance predicate, or
audit agreement threshold fails. Required artifacts are cards, hashes, manifests,
replay logs, and coded ledgers.

\paragraph{H10: Learned and language-model fidelity.}
Compare learned or language-model generators with declared baselines under an equal
budget vector: offline data and compute; online calls, tokens, and wall time; and
validation/repair cost, or a preregistered conversion. Estimate constraint,
distributional, semantic-intent, and deterministic-reconstruction fidelity. Reject a
fidelity claim when any preregistered dimension fails. Test explanation and semantic
fidelity against generator traces and declared constraints using precision/recall or
intervention consistency, not subjective prose. Required artifacts are prompts,
traces, configurations, outputs, budgets, validators, and reconstruction tests.

\paragraph{H11: Maritime, 3D, and benchmark drift.}
Compare source-to-simulator conformance and policy outcomes for maritime navigability,
currents/wind, 3D gate visibility, and ordered traversal; rerun a fixed panel after
every version change. Reject coverage or maintenance claims on missing required
predicates, failed round trips, or unexplained version shifts. Required artifacts are
domain contracts, simulator mappings, versioned suites, compatibility records, and
deprecation logs.
