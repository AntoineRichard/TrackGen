\section{Open Problems}
\label{sec:open-problems}

The following directions are benchmark questions with possible refutations, rather
than generic future work. Their outcome measures should retain raw feasibility and
repair lineage from \cref{sec:metrics}, and use the immutable-suite and paired-policy
procedures in \cref{sec:benchmark-protocol}.

\subsection{Representation and Feasibility}

\paragraph{Cross-domain IR.}
Can one \texttt{CourseSpec} core preserve ground contact/boundaries, aerial
three-dimensional gate traversal/visibility, and maritime navigability without lossy
simulator-specific fields? Test round-trip export/import across domain adapters against
source predicates; systematic loss in any required predicate rejects the common core.

\paragraph{Geometry versus dynamics.}
How much do geometry- and topology-valid courses overstate feasibility under declared
vehicle, actuator, and control envelopes? Compare geometric and dynamics/control-aware
yield across matched spectrum strata. A bounded negligible gap supports a geometry-only
approximation for that envelope; a structured gap refutes it.

\paragraph{Difficulty calibration.}
Can a difficulty spectrum be calibrated independently of policy class? Test rank
stability of descriptor strata and policy-derived outcomes across reference agents,
training seeds, and simulators. An ordering that reverses is policy or simulator
conditioned, not a general difficulty scale.

\subsection{Generation Objectives and Feedback}

\paragraph{Representative versus adversarial generation.}
Can a generator match a declared target distribution while exposing rare feasible
failures? Evaluate held-out distribution distance, coverage, duplicate rate, and policy
failure modes separately. Moving outside preregistered representative bounds while
improving an adversarial objective rejects representative-generation claims.

\paragraph{UED and curriculum feedback.}
When generator and policy co-adapt, does the curriculum generalize beyond its feedback
loop? Freeze generator snapshots and evaluate on common held-out generators, seeds, and
simulators. Gains confined to the co-adapted distribution falsify transfer; raw attempt
and repair logs show whether feedback merely selects easier valid cases.

\paragraph{Repair bias.}
Does repair shift descriptors or suppress failure modes enough to alter comparisons?
Measure paired raw-to-repaired changes in predicates, spectrum membership, diversity,
and policy outcomes. Changes outside preregistered tolerances require separate raw and
repaired conclusions.

\subsection{Transfer and Underrepresented Domains}

\paragraph{Cross-generator and cross-simulator transfer.}
Do policies trained on one generator family or simulator retain performance on portable
courses from another, and does the IR predict transfer loss? Use a crossed
generator-by-simulator matrix with common course identities. Material off-diagonal loss
rejects a generalization claim and can isolate representation, adapter, and policy
contributions.

\paragraph{Maritime and 3D challenges.}
Can benchmarks cover maritime routes and three-dimensional gates with sufficient
semantic fidelity to test control rather than importer artifacts? Require navigable
regions, current/wind, visibility, and ordered-gate predicates, then compare
source-to-simulator conformance and policy failures. Missing maritime suites or failed
3D gate/visibility round trips are measurable coverage gaps.

\subsection{Learned Generators and Governance}

\paragraph{Learned and language-model generators.}
Can learned or language-model-driven generators satisfy constraints and produce an
auditable decision trace under equal budgets? Test raw constraint yield,
invalid-output taxonomy, reconstruction from prompt/configuration/seed, and explanation
fidelity to the emitted \texttt{CourseSpec}. Unreconstructible outputs or untraceable
constraint changes reject auditability claims even when selected courses are valid.

\paragraph{Benchmark governance and drift.}
Can a benchmark remain comparable as exporters, simulators, descriptors, and policies
change? Version suites, cards, adapters, and reference normalizations; rerun a fixed
conformance and policy panel after each change; and publish compatibility and
deprecation records. A material unexplained shift demonstrates drift; reproducible
replay and explicit version splits support long-term maintenance.
