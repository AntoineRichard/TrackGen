3. **Deciding locators and fact.**
   - p. 1, Figure 1 caption and Abstract: the pipeline uses a tensor field and
     editing operations "to generate a road network," and the framework lets a
     user create or modify a street network.
   - p. 2, Section 3, *Pipeline Overview*, Stage Two: the paper defines a
     street-graph generation step; streets are computed as hyperstreamlines,
     and the network is stored as graph `G = (V, E)` with road width, type,
     markings, and lane attributes at nodes and edges.
   - p. 6, Section 6.1, *Generating Major Street Graph*: intersections of
     major and minor hyperstreamlines form graph vertices and consecutive
     segments form graph edges. Section 6.2 then generates a minor graph from
     the major graph; p. 7, Section 6.4 describes replacing an existing local
     street network with a tensor-field-generated one.

The deciding fact is therefore direct and source-native: this report generates
and edits connected, attributed road-network geometry rather than merely
describing roads. Its transferable-adjacent-domain mapping is explicit: the
generated graph provides connected route topology, while its road-width and
road-segment attributes provide traversable corridor geometry; those are course
constraints that materially determine a ground racing robot's traversal.
